Lemon

xli
2021-08-27
Plant

Classification

Class Order Family Genus
Eudicots Sapindales Rutaceae Citrus

Basic Information

  • Flower Color: White and Yellow
  • Bloom Time: December - March
  • Native Area: Southern Asia
  • Germination Period: 2 Weeks
  • Toxic: No

Evaluation

Item Score Note
Flower 7/10 More or less purple before blossom, gradually becomes pure white with a yellow core, spreading lemon odor.
- - - - - -
Leaf 9/10 Oval-shaped leaves, dark green and shiny and thick. Slightly oily with an aromatic, when squeezing, spread a strong lemon flavor.
- - - - - -
Odor 10/10 Stem, leaf, flower, and fruit, all of them has a light, cool, and clear-headed smell.
- - - - - -
Disease/Pest 2/5 Not easy to attract insects, but is prone to diseases.
- - - - - -
Easy to Grow 2/5 Control the temperature and humidity. Take care to prevent diseases.
- - - - - -
Usage +5 Fruits, flowers, and leaves can be used in cooking and flavoring.
- - - - - -

Tips

Temperature

Keep the temperature higher than 5 °C.

Humidity

Water it frequently to keep the soil moist, approximately twice or three times per week. When it needs water, new axillary buds will become soft and weak. However, mature leaves remains in the common status, so it is not easy to determine whether it needs water from mature ones.

Oil Gland of Rutaceae

Members from this family have a type of oil gland, which are found in the leaves responsible for the aromatic smell.
